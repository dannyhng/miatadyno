How to Get Good Data

The Pull

  1. Engine fully warm — drive at least 10 min first
  2. Pick 2nd gear — pulls from ~20 to ~60 MPH
  3. Slow to 20 MPH, ~2,000 RPM
  4. Press throttle to the floor — hold it there
  5. Hold until 6,500 RPM then lift off
  6. Coast down. Don't brake hard immediately
  7. Repeat once more — 2 pulls is better than 1

What to Avoid

  • Don't shift mid-pull — one gear only
  • Avoid hills and on/off-ramps with a slope
  • Don't pull into a headwind if you can help it
  • Skip the run if road is wet or cold (<40°F)
  • Wait for traffic to clear — don't rush it

Most of the OBD scanners log at ~1–2 Hz. Fewer PIDs logged = faster polling = cleaner curve.

Before You Run

MiataDyno estimates wheel horsepower using road load physics applied to your OBD log data. Results are estimates — not certified dyno figures. Accuracy depends on pull quality, road conditions, and how well your build is configured.

To collect data, you'll need to do a wide-open throttle acceleration run. This must only be done on private property, a closed course, or a legal road where it is safe and lawful to do so. Never exceed posted speed limits. Never endanger yourself, passengers, or other road users. MiataDyno does not encourage reckless driving — you are solely responsible for how you use this tool.
